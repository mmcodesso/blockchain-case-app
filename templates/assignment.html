Assignment: Blockchain Technology in Audit Practice

Context:

As emerging technologies continue to shape the accounting industry, blockchain stands out for its potential to revolutionize the way transactions are recorded and verified. This technology offers auditors a new tool to enhance the audit process. In this assignment, you will interact with a simple blockchain application that records transactions of students enrolling in courses.

Instructions:

  1. Understand the Application: Begin by studying the Python code for the Blockchain and Block classes, and the Flask application. This application uses a simplified model of a blockchain to record transactions. Each transaction should include the names of a student and professor, and the name of a course.
  2. Interact with the Blockchain: Use the form provided in the application to add new transactions to the blockchain. Note how each block added to the blockchain is immutable, meaning once a transaction has been recorded, it cannot be altered without changing all subsequent blocks.
  3. Audit the Blockchain: Act as an auditor verifying the transactions recorded on the blockchain. Check the integrity of the blockchain by ensuring that the "previous_hash" of each block matches the "hash" of the previous block. This is one way auditors can verify the integrity of transactions recorded on a blockchain.
  4. Identify Anomalies: Suppose an anomaly was detected in the blockchain. Discuss the steps you would take to investigate the issue. Consider how the immutability of the blockchain affects your investigation.

Discussion Questions:

  1. How can the immutability of blockchain transactions improve the audit process?
  2. In what scenarios might blockchain technology be particularly useful in auditing?
  3. What challenges might auditors face when auditing transactions recorded on a blockchain?

Rubric:

  • Understanding of Blockchain Technology (30 points): Clear understanding of how the blockchain application works and its implications for auditing.
  • Interaction with the Blockchain (30 points): Successful interaction with the blockchain application, including adding transactions and checking the integrity of the blockchain.
  • Identification of Anomalies (20 points): Thoughtful discussion of how to investigate a hypothetical anomaly detected in the blockchain.
  • Discussion Questions (20 points): Insightful responses to the discussion questions, demonstrating a solid understanding of the implications of blockchain technology for auditing.

Remember to include your findings and thought process in a written report. Your grade will depend on the depth of your understanding, the quality of your work, and the insights you provide in your report. Good luck!
